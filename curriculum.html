Lesson 1: Intro to Python

Students will learn what a computer programming language is, why learning Python is worthwhile, and touch upon basic syntax in Python. Print statements, math operations, variables, and types will all be covered in this lesson. By the end of Lesson 1, students will be able to print a random number.


Lesson 2: Conditionals and Logic

Students will learn about if statements, else statements, and elif statements. They will also learn the meaning of "and" and "or" in computer logic. By the end of Lesson 2, students will have a completed Rock, Paper, Scissors game.


Lesson 3: Loops

Students will learn about for and while loops and the logic behind both. They will practice using both in applicable settings, and by the end of Lesson 3, will have a complete Random Number Guessing game that they can play against the computer.


Lesson 4: Lists and Dictionaries

Students will learn about what lists and dictionaries are in Python, as well as their applications. To practice using lists, as well as the methods that can be performed on lists, students will practice making grocery lists.


Lesson 5: Methods and Classes

Students will learn about the anatomy of methods and classes, and practice writing their own of each. They will learn what parameters and method calls are, and by the end of Lesson 5, they will have a completed MadLib Generator.


Lesson 6: Graphics

Students will learn how graphics can enhance a video game, as well as how to use graphics in Python, using PyGame. By the end of Lesson 6, they will have a solid graphics foundation, as well as a graphic Rock, Paper, Scissors game, and enhanced version of the previously text-based one.


Final Project

After students have progressed through the first 6 lessons, they will be guided through creating their own game: Treasure Hunter. Much like battleship, players must find 3 gold nuggets on a 5 by 5 grid before their turns are up. This project utilizes a little bit of every lesson, and will allow students to walk away with a concrete game they created themselves.
